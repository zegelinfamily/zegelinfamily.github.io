Prawn And Prosecco Linguine

home

Serves 2
This recipe originally used crab meat but prawn is just as good
Source: Gino’s Italian Adriatic Escape

Timing is important. Linguine and prawn mixture need to be ready at the same time

Ingredients:

  • 250g dried linguine
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, peeled and finely chopped
  • 1 fresh, medium-hot red chilli, deseeded and finely chopped
  • 100g fresh red cherry tomatoes, halved
  • Zest of 1 unwaxed lemon
  • 150ml prosecco
  • 200g fresh prawns or white crab meat
  • 60g pitted green olives, drained and halved
  • 3 tbsp chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley
  • salt

Preparation:

  1. Cook the linguine in a pan of boiling, salted water until al dente.
  2. Put the oil, garlic and chilli in a large, high-sided frying pan or sauté pan and place over a medium heat
  3. Fry gently for 1 minute, stirring constantly
  4. Add the tomatoes and cook for 2 minutes until slightly soft
  5. Stir in the lemon zest and season with salt. Increase the heat and pour in the prosecco
  6. Bring to the boil and let it bubble for 1-2 minutes. Add the prawns, olives and parsley and turn off the heat
  7. When the linguine is cooked, drain slightly, leaving some water. Add prawn mixture
  8. Toss the pan to ensure the pasta is evenly coated and serve immediately.
