Cosmopolitan

home

The origin of the Cosmopolitan is disputed but its creation is usually attributed either to bartender Neal Murray in 1975 at the Cork and Cleaver Restaurant in Minneapolis or to bartender Cheryl Cook in 1985 at the Strand Restaurant in South Beach, Florida.

The use of citrus flavoured vodka as the basis for this cocktail was widely popularized in the mid 1990s and is used in the International Bartenders Association (IBA) approved recipe. However, many bartenders continue to use a standard unflavoured vodka and this alternative is historically consistent with the versions of this drink that were popular during the 1970s.

A lemon or lime twist is sometimes used as a garnish.

Ingredients:

  • 40ml vodka
  • 15ml Cointreau
  • 15ml fresh lime juice
  • 30ml cranberry juice
  • OR
  • 2 parts vodka
  • 1 part Cointreau
  • 2-3 parts cranberry juice
  • squeezed lime

Preparation:

  1. Shake all the ingredients in a cocktail shaker filled with ice
  2. Strain into a large cocktail glass
  3. Optional: garnish with a lemon or lime twist
